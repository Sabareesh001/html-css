A country is a distinct territorial, political, and geographical unit, generally defined by having a defined territory, a permanent population, a government, and sovereignty. As a recognized state, it operates independently, often holding membership in international bodies like the United Nations, and maintains control over its borders, laws, and economy.

A continent is a large, continuous landmass that is typically separated by oceans and distinguished by geographical, cultural, and historical factors. The seven continents are Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, and Australia. Each continent has its own unique features, including diverse ecosystems, climates, and human populations. Continents play a crucial role in shaping global geography and human civilization.

A city is a large human settlement that is typically characterized by a high population density, well-developed infrastructure, and a significant economic and cultural role within its region. Cities often serve as centers of commerce, industry, and governance, and they can vary greatly in size, history, and cultural significance.
